# Examples — Agency & Freelance

## Micro-Insights

```
Clients don't pay for hours. They pay for outcomes.
Scope creep starts with "just one small thing."
The cheapest client is always the most expensive.
Good briefs prevent bad revisions.
Retainers beat projects every time.
Your portfolio is your sales team.
Saying no is a business strategy.
The client who haggles on price will haggle on everything.
Clear contracts prevent awkward conversations.
Referrals close faster than cold outreach.
```

## Relatable List

```
Freelancing in 2026:
- Client asks for "just a few changes."
- Send revision 1.
- They say it's perfect.
- Two days later: "actually can we go back to the original?"
- It's revision 7.
- The deadline was last week.

Tag the freelancer who's currently on revision 11 of a "quick project."
```

```
Agency new business call in 2026:
- Great conversation.
- They love the work.
- Perfect fit.
- "We just need to align internally."
- Radio silence for 3 weeks.
- "We decided to go a different direction."

Tag the account manager who has 6 of these in the pipeline right now.
```

## Call-Out

```
This is for the freelancer who keeps lowering their rate to win projects.
The clients you attract at that price are the reason you want to raise it.
```

```
This is for the agency owner who hasn't raised prices in two years.
Your costs went up. Your rates didn't.
```

## Questions

```
What would happen to your business if your biggest client left tomorrow?
```

