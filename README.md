# Threads B2B Growth Playbook

A simple, repeatable content system for growing a B2B audience on Threads — without paid ads, viral gimmicks, or posting 10x a day.

Works best for: SaaS, agencies, service businesses, and niche B2B brands.

---

## The Core Idea

Most B2B accounts on Threads either over-explain their product or post generic "thought leadership" that nobody saves or shares. This playbook is built around three post formats that consistently drive engagement and follows — especially in niche markets.

---

## The 3 Formats

### 1. Micro-Insight (use 3–5x per day)

One sentence. One idea. Done.

The goal is frictionless engagement — someone reads it in 3 seconds and hits like without thinking. The Threads algorithm reads early likes as a signal to push the post further.

**Formula:**
```
[Concept] + [Outcome]
[Action] = [Result]
[Subject] does [thing] every time.
```

**Rules:**
- Max 8 words
- No hashtags
- End with a period — it signals finality
- No "I think" or "maybe" — only confident statements
- Post each one separately, never as a list

**Examples:**
```
Your email list is yours. The algorithm isn't.
Most lists die from silence, not unsubscribes.
One expired permit closes everything.
Restaurants don't fail on food. They fail on margins.
The fine is always bigger than the fee.
Nobody becomes a chef to manage a spreadsheet.
```

---

### 2. Relatable List Post (use 1x per 5–7 days)

A short story in bullet form that describes a painful, familiar situation — followed by an ironic contrast. Ends with "Tag someone who…"

This format gets saves and tags, which signals the algorithm that the content is worth amplifying.

**Formula:**
```
[Topic] in [year]:
- [Step 1]
- [Step 2]
- [Step 3]
- [Ironic or painful step]
- [The depressing reality]

Meanwhile / But / And yet: [contrast]

Tag the [specific person] who [recognizes themselves].
```

**Example:**
```
Email marketing in 2026:
- Open Mailchimp.
- Stare at blank subject line.
- Close Mailchimp.
- Tell yourself you'll do it Friday.
- It's been 3 months.

Meanwhile your competitors just hit send and booked 12 clients.

Tag the business owner who's been "almost ready to start" since January.
```

**Example 2:**
```
Restaurant permit management in 2026:
- Receive renewal notice.
- Put it in the "important" pile.
- Forget about the pile.
- Inspector shows up on a Friday night.
- Suddenly remember the pile.

Tag the owner who's currently looking for a permit they definitely renewed.
```

---

### 3. Call-Out Post (use 1–2x per week)

Speak directly to one specific person from the first line. Make them feel seen before they finish reading. No list, no contrast — just a direct, honest observation.

This is the most personal format. It works because the reader tags themselves mentally before they even reach the end.

**Formula:**
```
This is for the [who] who [what they're experiencing].
[One sentence that deepens it.]
[Optional: what they should do instead, or what's actually true.]
```

**Examples:**
```
This is for the business owner who has 800 subscribers and hasn't emailed them once this year.
The list isn't the problem. The silence is.
```

```
This is for the restaurant owner who worked 14 hours yesterday and still went home stressed about tomorrow.
You didn't open a restaurant to feel like this every night.
```

```
This is for the founder who rewrote the subject line 11 times and still didn't send it.
Done beats perfect. Especially in email.
```

---

### 4. Question Post (use 1x per week)

One honest, slightly uncomfortable question. No explanation. No context. Just the question.

The best question posts make people realize they don't have a good answer — and that discomfort makes them comment.

**What works:**
- Questions that expose a contradiction
- Questions where the honest answer is uncomfortable
- Questions directed at the reader personally (use "your", "you")

**Examples:**
```
If your email list disappeared tomorrow, would your business notice?
```
```
Would you subscribe to your own email list?
```
```
If you unsubscribed from your own newsletter tomorrow — would you miss anything?
```

## Posting Cadence

| Format | Frequency | Hashtags |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| Micro-insight | 3–5 per day | None |
| Relatable list | Once per 5–7 days | 3–5 broad ones |
| Call-out | 1–2 per week | None |
| Question | Once per week | None |

---

## What Not To Do

- Don't explain your product in micro-insights
- Don't add a CTA to every post
- Don't use hashtags on every post — it kills organic reach on Threads
- Don't bundle 5 micro-insights into one post — each one is a separate post
- Don't post the same format every day — mix it up

---

## Why This Works on Threads Specifically

Threads rewards early engagement velocity. A micro-insight that gets 10 likes in the first 10 minutes gets pushed to a wider audience. The formats in this playbook are designed to minimize the friction between "reading" and "engaging" — so the algorithm works for you instead of against you.

B2B accounts benefit especially because the niche is tight. When someone in your industry reads a micro-insight that nails their daily frustration in 7 words, they don't just like it — they follow.
